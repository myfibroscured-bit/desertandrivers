The people behind Desert & River

Desert & River is built around lived experience, deep respect for Sinai, and a slower way of travelling. Read a little about Annet, our shared story, and Joseph below.

Annet

Annet came to Sinai from the Netherlands and discovered in the desert something she could not ignore: silence, history, and a feeling of coming alive in a completely different way.

What began as a visit quickly became something much deeper. Under the stars, among ancient places and wide open landscapes, she felt a profound connection to the land and its stories. That experience drew her back to Egypt, where her love for the desert became part of a shared life and shared work.

Today, Annet helps shape journeys that are thoughtful, personal, and rooted in genuine care for people, animals, and place.

Our Shared Story

We met in the desert, and what began as one journey became a shared life built around history, guiding, and a belief that desert travel should be slower, kinder, and more respectful.

Over the years, we built our experience through guiding, hospitality, research, and long relationships with the people and landscapes of Sinai. We saw what rushed tourism often misses: the stillness, the inscriptions, the old routes, the responsibility, and the need to care properly for animals and land.

Desert & River grew from that understanding. We wanted to create journeys that allow people to experience the desert deeply rather than simply pass through it.

Joseph

Joseph’s love of history began young and developed into a lifetime of reading, learning, exploring, and guiding people through the hidden stories of Egypt and Sinai.

From difficult beginnings and hard work as a child, he built his own path through determination and a deep hunger to learn. Books opened the world first, then travel, then the desert itself. In Sinai he found a place where silence, history, and landscape all speak together.

That long personal journey now shapes the way he guides: with knowledge, patience, humour, and real respect for the people and places that make Sinai what it is.
