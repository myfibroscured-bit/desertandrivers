A practical packing list for Sinai desert journeys

Feb 2026 • 10 min

Packing for the desert is mostly about comfort and self-respect. You don’t need lots of gear — you need the right basics, chosen calmly.

Core clothing (works for most seasons)

  • Lightweight long sleeves (sun protection without overheating)
  • Loose trousers (movement, breathability, respect)
  • A warm layer for evenings (desert temperatures drop)
  • A wind layer (especially in open areas)
  • A scarf or buff (dust, wind, sun)

Footwear

Bring one pair you trust. Blisters ruin good days.

  • Closed-toe walking shoes with grip
  • Optional sandals for camp

Personal essentials

  • Sun protection (hat + sunscreen)
  • Headlamp or small torch
  • Reusable water bottle
  • Basic personal medications
  • Wet wipes / small towel

Comfort items (optional)

  • A small notebook
  • Earplugs (wind can be loud)
  • A simple camera if you prefer not to use your phone

What not to bring

  • Anything fragile you’ll worry about
  • Outfits for “content”
  • Excess gear that becomes a burden

We’ll advise based on your itinerary

This is a general list. Once we know your duration and places, we’ll advise what’s useful and what’s unnecessary so you travel light and ready.
